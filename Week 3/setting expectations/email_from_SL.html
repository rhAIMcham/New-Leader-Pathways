Hi New Leader,

I wanted to check in on how your first week has gone leading the team. Still feeling good, I hope.

As you reflect on the week, it would be useful to hear what you’ve learned so far about the team, and any early patterns you’ve noticed that you might want to change or adjust in the future.

Using your completed DISC profile, answer the questions below:

  • How confident did you feel leading the team?
  • How effectively did you communicate with others, and how did your team members respond to your approach?
  • How was stability and trust built within the team during this period? Where do you think the team might need more reassurance?
  • Where might there need to be more structure or attention to detail?
  • Did you notice your natural leadership style appear when completing the activities? How might you adapt your approach to meet the different needs of your team?

Susan H to New Leader

12:03 pm

Hey New Leader!

Congratulations on the first day in your new role. This is an exciting milestone and recognition of the commitment you have demonstrated so far. As you begin this transition, it is important to remember that moving from team member to leader is one of the most significant changes you will experience in your professional life.

It is normal for this period to feel disruptive, so reflection and growth will be crucial. Relationships will change, expectations will increase, and you may have to assess situations from a completely new perspective. Think about how you want to lead your new team and then answer the questions below. They will help me evaluate what assistance or other resources you may need during the first few weeks. Please be honest!

About you:

  • What qualities do you want to incorporate into your own leadership style?
  • What excites you about this role, and what concerns you?

Your team:

  • How might your colleagues feel about your role as a new leader?
  • How will you build trust with each team member?

Potential challenges:

  • What support might you need from your manager during this transition?
  • After reading about your team in the previous lesson, are there any problems that stand out?

I look forward to supporting you throughout your leadership journey.


Talk soon.

Susan
